How the Roleplay Cafe came to be

In 2014, the 5th Edition of Dungeons and Dragons, one of the most popular roleplaying game, was released. At the same time a group of voice actors and friends were streaming their home game on Twitch.

The combination of these two things created a perfect storm: Dungeons and Dragons became popular once more, and with it other tabletop roleplaying games as well.

Many more people wanted to learn about roleplaying games and join the community, but it can seem quite daunting. Questions such as: which roleplaying game should I play, do I really need to read all those books, do I need to put on a voice, do I really need all those minis are very common.

This is why, in 2020 (the year of two Natural Twenties!), the Roleplay Cafe was opened: to serve as a homebase for a growing community of roleplayers, a place for comraderie and for bringing new people into the hobby.

The Roleplay Cafe takes care of all the logistics: a lace to play, food and drinks, minis and terrain, even beamers and sound systems. All you have to worry about is playing the game!

Richard, Roleplay Cafe owner

Richard learned about roleplaying games while still in high school, when the current edition of Dungeons and Dragons was 3.5. He briefly tried to get a game going, but couldn't find the right people.

At university he discovered the Call of Cthulhu roleplaying game, and both played and gamemastered with gusto.

It was with the release of Dungeons and Dragons 5th edition and the subsequent explosion that Richard decided to do more with roleplaying games.

He quickly realized that what people needed was a homebase, a community where they could get to know like-minded people and not have to worry about things like paying for minis, arranging for food and drinks, or even having a space for playing.

So together with his wife, he started the Roleplay Cafe, with the aim of turning it into a hub for roleplayers.

Liz, Roleplay Cafe owner

Liz had always had nerdy interests - video games, anime, comics - and also an interest in hospitality: interacting with customers, making them feel at home, giving them advice about food and drinks.

Her husband, Richard, introducted her to roleplaying games, and she has been playing them for years now. She still has fond memories of her first Paranoia game and getting into playfights with her fellow players.

She actually came up with the idea of the Roleplay Cafe, combining her husband's interest in roleplaying games with her own love of work in the hospitality sector.

She is the face of Roleplay Cafe, making sure that players and gamemasters are satisfied with their experience gaming at the cafe.
